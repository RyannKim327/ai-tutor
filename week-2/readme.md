# Lesson: Identifying Prime Numbers

## Introduction
In this week’s lesson, we will explore prime numbers. A prime number is like a unique individual that doesn’t easily make friends. It only connects with two numbers: itself and 1. For example, 2, 3, 5, and 7 are prime because they cannot be divided by any other numbers evenly.

Prime numbers are important in many areas of mathematics and computer science, especially in coding and security.

## Real-life Analogy
Imagine you have a box of chocolates, and each chocolate represents a number. A prime chocolate can only be shared with one other chocolate (1) and itself. Any chocolate that can be paired with another type of chocolate (like 4 can pair with 2) isn’t unique, hence not prime!

## Steps to Think
1. **Understand the problem:** You need to check if a given number is prime.
2. **Identify boundary cases:** Remember, numbers less than 2 aren’t prime.
3. **Logic Flow:** If you find any number that evenly divides the given number (other than 1 or itself), it’s not prime.
4. **Efficiency:** You don’t need to check all the way up to the number itself—just to its square root.
