# Counting Under Pressure

In your daily life, counting happens all the time. For instance, when you count the number of times you see a certain type of car on the street, you keep a tally of how many you have encountered. This week, we will explore counting through programming, which is crucial for understanding how things work together.

### Concept Explanation
When you encounter numbers, you may want to count how many times each number appears. Imagine you're at a birthday party, and every time someone says a favorite ice cream flavor, you want to remember how many times each flavor is mentioned. This is exactly what we will build!

### Challenge Details
In this challenge, you will build a counting mechanism. Your system will listen for number inputs and keep track of how many times you've called out numbers. At the end, you'll be able to see a summary of all the numbers and how many times each one has been mentioned.

### Steps to Apply Learning Inside `challenge.py`
1. Begin by thinking about how to set up a way to count numbers.
2. Create a loop that will allow several inputs from yourself or others—just like counting flavors at a party.
3. As you add numbers, keep track of how many times each one comes up.
4. Finally, present your findings so you can summarize your party data.

### Estimated Completion Time
This challenge should take about 3-5 days to complete, allowing time for thinking through each step and implementing your counting logic.
