# Lesson: Classifying Numbers by Range

In our previous lesson, we looked at whether numbers were odd or even. Now, let’s build on that idea by categorizing numbers based on their size. 

### Concept Explanation
Think of numbers like people at a party:
- Some are small, like kids, who may represent numbers from 1 to 10.
- Some are medium-sized, like teenagers, from 11 to 50.
- Finally, we have adults, who could represent larger numbers from 51 to 100 or more.

By organizing numbers into categories, we make it easier to understand them better. 

### Your New Challenge
For this challenge, you will categorize numbers into small, medium, and large based on the criteria you define. 

### Steps to Think and Implement in 'challenge.py'
1. **Define the ranges**: Decide what numbers are considered small, medium, and large. You can do this by writing down the ranges you think fit best.
2. **Gather numbers**: Think about how you will take or represent the numbers you want to categorize.
3. **Classify each number**: Create a method to check which range each number falls into and then assign it to a category.
4. **Output the results**: Decide how you want to present the classified numbers. You could list them out or organize them in some way.

### Estimated Completion Time
This challenge should take about 4–6 days to complete, giving you enough time to really think through each part of the process. 

Through this task, you will practice structured thinking—just like sorting items in your room into different boxes, you will learn to classify numbers logically within your program.
